# adi
Amok Drive Inhibition System
This repository contains the work products of a system to mitigate vehicle ramming attacks by means of an in-vehicle system.

Here is a short history of the idea.

2016:
I started to construct this idea after the vehicle ramming attacks in the years 2015-2016.

An initial assessment of the idea prove to face unsurmountable burdens in productization of the system because of the established regulations in the automotive industry. 

However the idea was interesting in many ways:
1) It is implementable with hardware components existing in most cars as standard feature.
2) It brings a different perspective in the safety


in 2023-2024
My team at DXC Luxoft performs ECU architecture assessments and I had to upskill my team members in functional safety (FuSa) and cybersecurity (CybSec) practice.
However our current project does not have FuSa and CybSec as a main responsibility.
Because of this reason, we were lacking detailed use cases where we could make exercises of engineering rigor on the practice of functional safety and cybersecurity methods.
So I pulled this old idea of mine out of the shelf and started to prepare it as a practice case for my team. 

2025:
At the beginning of the new year I decided to make the outcome of this work public to be able to be beneficial for the general public.
Therefore I started this repository and preparing documents to report the specification of ADI in a conference.

In later stages this application will not only serve as an learning ground for emerging experts in the field of Fusa and CybSec, but may become an industrialization ready open source solution.

I invite the research community to take the idea and bring it further with critics, contributions and recommendations. Please simply get in touch with me if you have any questions.
