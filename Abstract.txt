Since the first recorded incident in 1949, vehicle ramming attacks (VRAs) have resulted in over 400 fatalities and more than 2,100 injuries worldwide. 
A common preventive measure involves the physical separation of roadways and pedestrian areas using Vehicle Security Barriers (VSBs), such as concrete blocks or retractable bollards. 
While effective, these passive, location-specific solutions cannot fully prevent VRAs, as attacks can occur anywhere. 
A more comprehensive approach requires an in-vehicle system that travels with the vehicle, ensuring protection regardless of location. 
In this study, we propose and evaluate an active in-vehicle system designed to mitigate VRAs by restricting the buildup of excessive kinetic energy when malicious intent is suspected. 
This novel approach offers a dynamic, location-independent solution to counteract VRAs at their onset.
